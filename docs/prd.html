Executive Summary

Ardent Mills operates 10 milling facilities across the United States, processing and shipping wheat, corn, soy, and oat products to bakeries and food manufacturers nationwide. Today, operations teams rely on disconnected spreadsheets, email threads, and phone calls to track shipment status, monitor mill performance, and manage grain quality certifications.

The Supply Chain Dashboard is a proof-of-concept web application that consolidates this data into a single, real-time interface. It gives supply chain managers, mill operators, and quality analysts instant visibility into the health of the entire distribution network — from grain leaving a mill to delivery confirmation at a customer bakery.

Product Vision

Provide every Ardent Mills team member with a single source of truth for supply chain operations — enabling faster decisions, earlier problem detection, and a measurable improvement in on-time delivery and quality compliance.

What This PoC Proves
  • Feasibility — A full-stack Angular 22 + ASP.NET Core 9 application can be built and deployed from a clean slate inside one sprint.
  • Architecture — Signal-based state, httpResource, and OnPush change detection work together cleanly for this domain.
  • UX — The Material M3 design system can be themed to Ardent Mills brand standards without heavy custom CSS work.
  • Data model — The four-entity schema (Mill, Shipment, MillAlert, QualityTest) covers the minimum viable data surface for meaningful supply chain visibility.
01

Context & Problem Statement

Current State

Supply chain visibility at Ardent Mills is fragmented across multiple systems with no unified operational view:

  • Shipment tracking is managed via carrier portals and email updates — no central status board.
  • Mill utilization is reported via weekly Excel sheets emailed by each facility's plant manager.
  • Quality data (protein, moisture, ash, falling number) lives in a legacy LIMS that lacks a modern API.
  • Alerts and incidents are communicated via phone and text, with no structured escalation path.
  • Managers must aggregate data from 4+ sources before answering any cross-mill question, typically taking 30–60 minutes.
Pain Points
Critical Pain

When a mill goes offline or a shipment is delayed, there is no system-level alert. Customers learn about delivery failures before internal managers do, damaging trust and creating reactive, expensive resolutions.

  • Average time-to-awareness for a mill outage: 4–6 hours (target: under 15 minutes).
  • Quality failures are discovered at delivery, not at dispatch — causing costly returns and re-shipments.
  • Executive KPI reports require manual compilation every Monday morning — a 2-hour task per analyst.
  • No historical trend data is readily available for capacity planning or carrier performance analysis.
Opportunity

A unified dashboard with real-time data integration eliminates the manual aggregation overhead, surfaces alerts at the point of origin, and provides a foundation for predictive analytics in future phases.

User Personas

👨‍💼
Primary User
Supply Chain Manager
Oversees shipments from all 10 mills to customer distribution centers. Responsible for on-time delivery KPIs and carrier relationship management. Checks the dashboard multiple times per day.
Goals
  • See all in-transit shipments and identify delays before customer contact
  • Quickly reroute shipments when a mill goes offline
  • Track carrier performance across rail, truck, and barge
  • Build weekly summary for leadership in under 10 minutes
Pain Points
  • Currently checks 5 different carrier portals for shipment status
  • No single view of all-mill utilization vs. capacity
🏭
Operational User
Mill Operations Manager
Manages day-to-day production at a single mill facility. Responsible for throughput, equipment uptime, and scheduling outbound shipments. Uses the dashboard primarily to view their own mill's status and outbound queue.
Goals
  • See real-time utilization vs. daily capacity target
  • Log and escalate maintenance alerts promptly
  • Track outbound shipments from their facility
  • View quality test results for batches they shipped
Pain Points
  • Cannot see how their output affects overall network supply
  • Quality failures reported back days after shipment
🔬
Specialist User
Quality Assurance Analyst
Runs and reviews quality tests on grain batches before they ship. Responsible for grading (US No.1, No.2, Sample) and ensuring specs are met. Reviews test history and identifies systematic issues at specific mills.
Goals
  • Filter quality tests by mill, grain type, result, or date range
  • Quickly identify which batches failed and why
  • Track trends in protein, moisture, and falling number by mill
  • Flag Sample Grade lots before they leave the facility
Pain Points
  • LIMS system has no cross-mill comparison view
  • Manual export to Excel required for any analysis
📊
Executive Viewer
VP Supply Chain / Leadership
Reviews weekly KPI summaries and approves strategic decisions (capacity investments, carrier contracts, mill maintenance windows). Uses the dashboard on a read-only basis for high-level trend data and exception reporting.
Goals
  • See network-level KPIs (total shipments, on-time %, quality score)
  • Understand which mills are underperforming and why
  • Track 12-month shipment volume trends
  • Present supply chain health to board without manual prep
Pain Points
  • KPI decks require 2 hours of manual data gathering each Monday
  • No real-time view — all reports are 1 week stale
04

Scope

✓ In Scope — Phase 1 (PoC)
  • Dashboard with 4 KPI cards, trend chart, grain-mix doughnut, alert strip
  • Shipments list with filtering (status, grain type, origin, destination)
  • Shipment detail view — metadata, status timeline, lot codes
  • Mills grid with status chip filters and utilization bars
  • Mill detail — stats, alert list, production bars, recent shipments
  • Quality tests table — protein/moisture/ash/FN, grade color coding, failures toggle
  • REST API with Swagger documentation
  • SQL Server LocalDB with seed data (10 mills, ~1,247 shipments, ~4,218 QC tests)
  • Angular Material M3 with Ardent Mills brand tokens
  • Responsive layout (desktop + tablet)
  • Bearer token authentication (static demo token for PoC)
✗ Out of Scope — Phase 1
  • Real-time carrier API integrations (UPS, BNSF, Coyote Logistics)
  • Production identity provider (Azure AD / Okta SSO)
  • Write operations — creating or editing shipments, mills, alerts
  • Email / SMS / Teams alert notifications
  • Mobile native application (iOS/Android)
  • LIMS system integration for live quality data
  • ERP / SAP integration for shipment creation
  • Predictive analytics / ML models
  • Multi-tenant or multi-company support
  • Audit logging and compliance reporting
  • Offline / PWA support
  • Data export (PDF, Excel download)
Phase 2 Preview

The PoC architecture is deliberately designed to accommodate Phase 2 additions without a rewrite — carrier webhooks will integrate via new API endpoints, SSO via Angular auth guards already stubbed, and write operations via new POST/PATCH controller actions on the existing service layer.

Risks & Mitigations

RiskLikelihoodImpactMitigation
ng2-charts v7 incompatibility with Angular 22 LOW HIGH Verified: ng2-charts v7 supports Angular 22 (uses Chart.js 4 natively). Fallback: drop to pure Chart.js canvas with manual integration.
Seed data generation performance MED MED Use HasData() for static reference data only. Generate bulk Shipments/QualityTests via a dedicated DataSeeder service that runs once on first startup rather than migration.
Signal Store learning curve (new API) MED LOW Architecture documents provide detailed code patterns. Start with ShipmentsStore as the reference implementation before copying the pattern to other stores.
ExpressionChangedAfterChecked errors from loading state HIGH MED Resolved by design: loadingInterceptor wraps signal writes in queueMicrotask() to defer past the current change detection cycle.
Scope creep (stakeholders request write features) HIGH HIGH Explicit out-of-scope list in this PRD. Redirect any write-operation requests to the Phase 2 backlog. PoC is read-only by design.
SQL Server LocalDB not available on developer machines LOW MED LocalDB ships with Visual Studio 2022. Fallback: Docker image mcr.microsoft.com/mssql/server:2022-latest with identical EF Core config.
Material M3 theme token conflicts with brand colors MED LOW Brand color tokens defined as CSS custom properties at :root level — override M3 tokens where necessary without modifying M3 internals.

Assumptions & Constraints

Assumptions
  • Single developer builds the full PoC. Timeline assumes 40 hours/week dedicated capacity.
  • Seed data is representative — the 10 mill names, KPI values (1,247 shipments, 96.4% quality score, 92.7% on-time), and quality spec thresholds have been confirmed with the operations team.
  • Read-only for PoC — stakeholders accept that the PoC cannot create, update, or delete records. All data mutations happen outside the dashboard during Phase 1.
  • Local development only — the PoC runs on localhost with SQL Server LocalDB. No cloud infrastructure is provisioned in Phase 1.
  • Demo token acceptable — a static Bearer token is sufficient for the PoC demonstration. Real Azure AD / Okta integration deferred to Phase 2.
  • No real carrier data — shipment status (InTransit, Delayed, etc.) and ETA dates are seeded statically. Live carrier API webhooks are Phase 2 scope.
  • English only — no i18n/l10n requirements for Phase 1.
Constraints
  • Budget constraint — No paid UI component libraries. Angular Material M3 is the only component framework (MIT licensed, free).
  • Technology constraint — Stack is fixed: Angular 22 + Angular Material + ng2-charts on the frontend; ASP.NET Core 9 + EF Core 9 + SQL Server on the backend. No substitutions.
  • No external CDNs in production — all assets must be bundled. Google Fonts acceptable for PoC/demo environment.

Open Questions

OQ-01
What is the correct quality spec threshold for Dockage % in wheat?
The mockup shows a Dockage% column in the quality table but no spec threshold has been confirmed by the QA team. Currently displayed as a raw number. Needed to implement the spec dot indicator for this field.
OQ-02
Should the "Failures Only" toggle also filter the 5 Quality KPI mini-cards, or only the table?
Current mockup shows the KPI strip (Total Tests: 4,218, Pass Rate: 96.4%) is static regardless of the toggle. It may be more useful to recompute these to match the current filter state.
OQ-03
What defines a "delayed" shipment — is it ETA exceeded, or carrier-reported delay event?
For the PoC, Status = Delayed is a static field on the Shipment entity. In production this may be a computed state derived from comparing current timestamp vs. ETA. Needs a business rule definition for Phase 2.
OQ-04
Should mill utilization % on the card be DailyOutput / CapacityTonsDay, or a rolling 7-day average?
Real-time utilization (today's output / max capacity) vs. a 7-day rolling average would give different readings. Current implementation uses same-day snapshot. Needs confirmation from operations team for accuracy requirements.
OQ-05
Who has authority to resolve a MillAlert, and should that action be available in Phase 1?
Currently alerts are read-only. The mockup shows resolved alerts in a "Resolved alerts history" section — but there is no write path to resolve them in Phase 1. Confirm whether a simple "Mark resolved" button should be added to scope.
OQ-06
What is the production hosting plan — Azure App Service, IIS, or containerized (Docker)?
The PoC runs locally. Phase 2 deployment target affects several infrastructure choices: whether to use Azure SQL vs. self-hosted SQL Server, whether to containerize the .NET API, and how Angular production builds are served (CDN vs. static web app).
